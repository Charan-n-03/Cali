Introduction

What is Calisthenics?

Calisthenics derives its name from two Greek words — kálos meaning beauty, and sthénos meaning strength. It is a discipline in which you train your entire body using only bodyweight exercises. No machines. No weights. Just you.

You may have seen people performing gravity-defying figures such as the Front Lever and Planche, or dynamic acrobatic skills on the bars like the Muscle Up. The exercises usually involve presses and pulls — push-ups, pull-ups, dips, squats — enhancing your body's strength, endurance, coordination, and flexibility.

What makes Calisthenics truly compelling is how it combines functionality and true bodyweight strength with an aesthetic, shredded physique. Not only will you develop raw muscle power, but also an impressive physical form that reflects genuine capability.

Why Train

Why Should You Train Calisthenics?

Even though I do not know you, I can almost surely say — Calisthenics is for you.

No Equipment Needed

Train wherever you like. You only need a bit of floor space and a pull-up bar. Calisthenics frees you from the gym.

Different Levels of Exercises

Hundreds of variations exist — from knee-assisted push-ups for beginners to one-arm push-ups for elite athletes. You will always find your challenge.

Weight Loss or Muscle Gain

With the right workout structure and nutrition, Calisthenics can help you lose weight or build serious muscle mass — the choice is yours.

Natural Body Strength

You train using natural movements, developing functional strength. Minor muscle groups, tendons, and joints all strengthen together.

Advanced Skills

Muscle Ups, Front Levers, Human Flags, Planches — with dedication and proper progression, these skills become achievable milestones.

Complete Body Transformation

Build confidence, meet inspiring people, and completely change how you look and feel. This is not just training — it is a lifestyle transformation.

Methods

Four Ways of Training

Different goals require different approaches. Your workout plan should be tailored to how advanced you are and what you want to achieve.

01

Hypertrophy Training

Build muscle mass through high-intensity resistance exercises with medium repetitions. Focus on progressive overload — each workout, do more than the last.

6–12 reps3–5 sets2–4 min restCaloric surplus
02

Maximum Strength

Increase raw power through neurological adaptations. Lower reps with heavier resistance — weighted pull-ups, weighted dips, and harder progression variations.

1–5 reps5–10 sets3–5 min restAdded weight
03

Skills Training

Learn impressive calisthenics skills — isometric holds (Front Lever, Planche) and dynamic moves (Muscle Up). Requires high frequency: 3–4 times per week minimum.

High frequency3–5 min restProgressionsGrease the groove
04

Muscular Endurance

Push your maximum repetition count. High volume, low resistance, minimal rest. Your mind works as hard as your body — push to the limit with perfect form.

12+ reps (MAX)5 cycles30 sec restPerfect form
Exercise Guide

Building Your Body

Every muscle group can be developed using bodyweight exercises. Here are the most effective movements for each body part.

Chest Dips

Bend forward with torso and legs. Keep elbows close to body, head neutral. Few exercises activate chest as much as this one.

PRIMARY: CHEST

Wide Push-Ups

Place hands wider than shoulder distance. Body straight, core squeezed. The wider the hands, the more chest activation.

PRIMARY: CHEST

Incline & Decline Push-Ups

Feet elevated targets upper chest. Hands elevated targets lower chest. Keep body straight and core engaged throughout.

UPPER & LOWER CHEST

Pull-Ups

Overhand grip, active hang with shoulders retracted. Wide grip emphasises lats, close grip targets lower lats. No kipping.

WIDE / NEUTRAL / CLOSE

Australian Pull-Ups

Inverted row with body straight below a bar. Ideal for building strength before full pull-ups. Experiment with different grips.

FOUNDATION BUILDER

Archery Pull-Ups

Keep one arm completely straight, pull yourself to the side of the working arm. Requires solid foundation.

ADVANCED

Handstand Push-Ups

Against the wall, body straight. Touch floor with nose, fully extend arms. Front deltoid focus. Build up with pike push-ups.

FRONT DELTOIDS

Bodyweight Face Pulls

Find a waist-high bar, hold like an Australian pull-up. Pull up to face level. Bodyweight equivalent of the classic face pull.

REAR DELTOIDS

Pseudo Planche Push-Ups

Lean forward from planche position, elbows close to body. Great for overall shoulder development.

GENERAL DELTOIDS

Straight Bar Dips

Dip on a single straight bar. Lean slightly forward, arms close to body. Prerequisite for Muscle Up.

GENERAL TRICEPS

Skull Crushers

Grab a waist-high bar with pull-up grip. Bend elbows until head touches bar, extend back. Targets the long head.

LONG HEAD

Bench Dips

Hands on a bench behind you, legs extended. Elbows close to body. Elevate legs for more challenge.

MEDIAL HEAD

Diamond Push-Ups

Diamond shape with fingers, elbows close to body. Engages triceps heavily. Go on knees if too challenging.

LATERAL HEAD

Chin-Ups

Palms facing towards you. Think of it as a bicep curl with your entire bodyweight. No momentum.

COMPOUND BICEPS

Bar Curls

Under a waist-high bar, chin-up grip. Focus on bicep curl movement — keep elbows in place.

ISOLATION

Behind-the-Bar Chin-Ups

Bar behind you, facing ground. More difficult but isolates biceps intensely.

ADVANCED ISOLATION

Toes to Bar

Hang in hollow body, raise legs until feet touch bar. Go past 90 degrees for maximum ab engagement.

LOWER ABS

Windshield Wipers

Pull back up parallel to ground, rotate torso so legs go parallel on each side. Great for obliques.

OBLIQUES

Plank

Elbows on ground, hollow body position. Compound core stabilizer. Move elbows further to increase difficulty.

CORE STABILIZER

L-Sit

On dip bars, lock elbows, shoulders down, legs straight. Builds core, shoulder, and tricep strength.

COMPOUND CORE

Squats

Heels shoulder-width, feet 30 degrees out. Full range past parallel. Add weight for muscle mass.

FOUNDATION

Calf Raises

Toes on elevated surface, raise and lower heels. Split: 6-12 weighted, 15-20 lighter reps.

CALVES

Lunges

Step forward, lower hips until 90 degrees. Add weight or try jumping lunges for intensity.

QUADS & GLUTES

Horizontal & Vertical Jumps

Jump as high or far as possible. Builds explosiveness and coordination. Always warm up first.

Every master was once a beginner. Here is the roadmap from your first push-up to elite-level skills.

Foundation Phase

BEGINNER

Focus on building a solid base of strength and learning proper form. Train Full Body 3 times per week.

  • • Knee Push-Ups → Regular Push-Ups
  • • Bench Dips → Assisted Dips
  • • Pike Push-Ups (for shoulders)
PULL EXERCISES
  • • Australian Pull-Ups
  • • Negative Pull-Ups
  • • Resistance Band Pull-Ups
CORE & LEGS
  • • Plank (30sec → 60sec)
  • • Knee Leg Raises
  • • Bodyweight Squats (3x15)
GOAL
  • • 15+ Regular Push-Ups
  • • 5+ Strict Pull-Ups
  • • 20+ Squats

Development Phase

INTERMEDIATE

Progress to harder variations and begin skill training. Train 4–5 times per week.

ADVANCED PUSH
  • • Decline & Wide Push-Ups
  • • Chest Dips, Straight Bar Dips
  • • Pseudo Planche Push-Ups
ADVANCED PULL
  • • Chin-Ups, L-Sit Pull-Ups

  • • Muscle Up Progressions
  • • Handstand Practice
  • • Back Lever Tuck Holds

  • • Clean Muscle Up
  • • 30s+ Handstand

Mastery Phase

Nutrition Principles

If you are working out towards a goal, you should eat accordingly. Here are the fundamental principles that actually matter.

Gain Muscle Mass

Calories In>Calories Out

Add 200–500 kcal to your daily maintenance. An average active male needs around 2500–3300 kcal.

Example: 3000 kcal + 400 kcal = 3400 kcal daily

Struggling to gain? Go 70% clean, 30% flexible. Some calorie-dense food helps.

Lose Body Fat

Calories In<Calories Out

Reduce 200–500 kcal from maintenance. Slow, consistent fat loss is sustainable fat loss.

Example: 2500 kcal - 400 kcal = 2100 kcal daily

Quality food keeps you full longer, making the deficit manageable.

Food Quality and Water

While calories dictate weight change, food quality affects how you feel. Quality food provides more volume per calorie. For those struggling to gain, some flexible eating is actually advisable — it easily delivers required calories and helps with adherence. Vitamins and micronutrients can be supplemented. Above all: consistency beats perfection.
